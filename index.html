Inside Her Dying Light

I lived inside her quiet ache, A shadow born from her slow break. Her silence hummed beneath my skin, A requiem that pulled me in. The sun bent down, its light half blind; It kissed her face, then left me behind. Its gold turned bruised, its warmth turned cold, A mercy cruel, a story told. She bled in silence, sweet, obscene, A prayer disguised in what had been. Her eyes were dusk, her breath was glass, Each heartbreak mourning what would pass. I felt her ending move through me, Like a dying star beneath the sea. She begged for peace; I felt, I stayed, Inside the light her death betrayed. She found her stillness; I, her pain. Her loss became my sweet domain. She sleeps in gold; I fade in grey, Alive where she dissolved away.

-- @cronus_dead
